# Homework: Build a Judge Persona

You made it through Module 1. This is the payoff: one small deep agent that uses everything you just learned, and hands you a result you can actually show off.

- **Answer** an 8-question quiz about how you work, arrow keys only, no typing.
- **Write** a judge with your own voice: rude, deadpan, gentle, chaotic-evil, whatever you want.
- **Get matched** to a real LangChain product based on your answers.
- **Approve** the post yourself before anything goes out. Nothing publishes without your say.
- **Walk away** with an ASCII result card in your terminal, ready to screenshot.

It's a genuinely fun build, and more fun once you compare cards with a classmate. Nobody's judge should sound the same.

<Tip>

This is the milestone. Everything before this module was reading and small labs; this is the first time you assemble the pieces yourself into something that's actually yours.

</Tip>

## What you'll build

Open `python/m1/homework/judge_card_homework.py`. Running it end to end:

1. Runs the arrow-key quiz.
2. Hands your answers to an agent running your persona.
3. The agent tallies your traits, matches you to a real LangChain product, and (stretch goal) grounds the verdict in a live fact instead of a placeholder.
4. You approve, edit, or reject the post before it goes anywhere.
5. Your approved card renders as ASCII art in your terminal and saves to `output/`.

## What's provided

The mechanical parts live in `judge_card_helpers.py`, same idea as `models.py`: shared setup you import, not code you need to read to do this homework.

- `run_quiz()`: the 8-question arrow-key quiz.
- `PRODUCT_MATCHES`: the trait -> real LangChain product lookup.
- `render_card()`: renders and saves your finished card as ASCII art.
- `post_card()`: the "publish" tool, mock by default, safe to run with no credentials.
- `run_judge()`: the invoke/interrupt-resume loop. You already wrote this once in the HITL lesson, no need to write it again.
- `TOOL_SEQUENCE`: the tool-calling steps every persona shares.
