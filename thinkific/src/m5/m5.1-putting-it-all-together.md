## The project: a sales assistant for Chinook

The capstone we'll build is a **sales assistant** for **Jane Peacock**, a sales rep at **Chinook** - an online music distributor. It helps her work her "book of business": it reads quote requests from her **Gmail** inbox, looks up customers and prices in the **Chinook sales database**, drafts replies for her to approve, writes a weekly music newsletter, and reports on how her territory is doing.

It's a single deep agent, but a complete, realistic application.

---

## Every feature, and where this project uses it

Here's the whole toolkit from the course, mapped to where the sales assistant puts each one to work:

- **Subagents**: `chinook-analyst` (owns the database), `inbox-manager` (owns Gmail), `quote-reviewer` (sanity-checks a quote), and `genre-researcher` (newsletter research). Each is a specialist with its own tools.
- **Parallel subagents**: the newsletter fans out one `genre-researcher` per genre, all at once, then stitches the segments together.
- **Skills + `AGENTS.md`**: a plain-text operating manual plus one playbook per task (RFQ quote, newsletter, territory report).
- **Filesystem**: the quotes ledger, the newsletter HTML page, and the territory report are written to disk.
- **Interpreter**: exact quote math: line totals and the volume discount, computed rather than guessed.
- **Sandbox** *(optional)*: chart generation for the territory report; without it, the report is numbers-only and everything else still works.
- **Human-in-the-loop**: approval gates on the two consequential actions: saving a Gmail draft and adding a new customer.
- **MCP**: Gmail access, through either a local mock server or real OAuth: the same tool surface either way.
- **Todo list**: sequencing the multi-step RFQ task so the agent keeps its place.

Over the rest of the module, you'll build this assistant and run it.
